Meowami City is a neon-soaked coastal metropolis built by cats ‚Äî and slowly consumed by corruption.

Once a glittering entertainment capital, the city has decayed into a layered battlefield of gangs, political control and crumbling glamour. Night never truly ends here ‚Äî it only shifts from electric pink to cold blue.

---

## Tone

- 80s arcade seriousness
- Neon contrast and urban decay
- Stylized, not realistic
- Grit without parody
- Energy without comedy
- Cat-themed cultural identity (subtle, not comedic)

---

## City Identity

- Dense vertical architecture
- Rooftops, alleys, underground clubs
- Industrial docks and abandoned districts
- Flickering signage and broken streetlights
- Fog mixed with sea air
- Cat-owned businesses with claw-marked signage
- Art deco facades with neon highlights

The city should feel alive ‚Äî but indifferent.

- Real estate and construction monopolies

## Conflict

Pigs and rats compete constantly:

- For districts
- For port access
- For nightlife revenue
- For narcotics and contraband routes

Their war reshapes neighborhoods.

But for ordinary citizens, nothing improves.

Prices rise.  
Businesses close.  
Streets grow dangerous.

War between predators does not create peace.

---

## Narrative Position

Four cats refuse to accept decay.

They are not heroes of the system.  
They are not aligned with pigs, rats or dogs.

They fight for:

- Their neighborhood
- Their people
- Their dignity

They are not saving Meowami.

They are taking it back ‚Äî one block at a time.
